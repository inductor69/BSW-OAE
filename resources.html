Visual impairments




Screen readers

Screen readers have been a blessing to accommodate people with visual impairments. These provide speech or braille as an output for most applications on computers. These days, a few of the computers have built-in screen readers, and there are several compatible software doing the job.

Screen Magnifiers

Screen Magnifiers are fully integrated magnification and reading programs tailored for low-vision users. Magnifier/Reader enlarges and enhances everything on your computer screen, echoes your typing and essential program activity, and automatically reads documents, web pages, email.

Video Magnifier

Video magnifiers are devices that use a camera and a display screen to perform digital magnification of printed materials. These come in handy for low-vision users while looking for details or specifics while looking at a printed document or just looking around.

Refreshable braille display

A refreshable braille display is an electro-mechanical device for displaying braille characters, usually employing round-tipped pins raised through holes in a flat surface. Visually impaired users unable to use a computer monitor can use it to read text output.

Tactile Graphics

Tactile graphics are images that use raised surfaces so that a visually impaired person can feel them. They are used to convey non-textual information such as maps, paintings, graphs, and diagrams. These empower the visually impaired, making them capable of understanding the mathematical/geographical and similar aspects connected to their learning.

Refreshable Graphics Display

A Refreshable Graphics Display is an Interactive Tactile Graphics Display which provides non-visual access to any form of graphical information such as charts, drawings, flowcharts, floorplans, images and photographs, through an array of moving pins. It also features a touch interface to enable the user to “draw” on the display; tracing a shape with a fingertip raises the pins along the path traced.

Accessible Scientific Editor

Accessible Scientific Editors are specifically designed mathematical document editors for visually disabled people to read and write scientific documents along with features of speech output of symbols and math structure. They support math expressions from secondary to university level mathematics.

Talking Scientific Calculator

Talking Scientific Calculator provides high-quality synthesized-speech that reads out all textual and symbolic information. Accompanied with unique audio access to graphs through speech output of graph parameters and has ability to output all text as braille script when connected with appropriate devices.

Hearing Impairments

Personal sound amplifying products are designed to boost people's environmental hearing without hearing loss. They aren't selective in what sounds they amplify and help the people accommodating hearing impairments.
